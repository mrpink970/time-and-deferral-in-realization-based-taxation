# Time Isn’t Free

## Time and Deferral in Realization-Based Taxation

**Public name:** Time Isn’t Free  
**Official technical title:** Time and Deferral in Realization-Based Taxation  
**Slogan:** Time Isn’t Free. Long-Term Tax Deferral Shouldn’t Be Either.

---

> **Note on naming:** “Time Isn’t Free” is the public-facing name. This document uses the technical title
> “Time and Deferral in Realization-Based Taxation” and focuses on pricing long-duration deferral within a realization system.

---

SECTION 0 — DEFINITIONS
For purposes of this Framework (“Time and Deferral in Realization-Based Taxation”), the following definitions apply.

0.1 Covered Taxpayer
A Covered Taxpayer means a household that meets the thresholds described in Section 1, determined based on aggregated assets and economic interests as provided in this Framework.

0.2 Household
Household means the Covered Taxpayer and any individuals whose assets, income, or economic interests are aggregated under the rules set forth in Section 4.

0.3 Covered Asset
A Covered Asset means any asset, interest, or position held directly or indirectly by a Covered Taxpayer. Covered Assets include, without limitation: (a) publicly traded securities; (b) privately held business interests; (c) real estate; (d) partnership or fund interests; and (e) derivative or synthetic positions that substantially replicate economic exposure to appreciation or depreciation of an underlying asset.

0.4 Covered Unrealized Appreciation (CUA)
Covered Unrealized Appreciation means, with respect to a Covered Asset, the excess of its fair market value over its adjusted tax basis, determined as of the applicable Measurement Date.

0.5 Deferral Cost Charge (DCC)
The Deferral Cost Charge is the annual charge described in Section 2 that reflects the economic benefit of deferring realization of Covered Unrealized Appreciation.

0.6 Deferral Duration / Deferral Clock
Deferral Duration (or deferral clock) means the number of consecutive taxable years during which Covered Unrealized Appreciation with respect to a specific Covered Asset has remained unrealized while subject to this Framework.

0.7 Time-Weighted Deferral Cost (TWDC)
The Time-Weighted Deferral Cost is the adjustment applied to the Deferral Cost Charge based on the length of the Deferral Duration for a specific Covered Asset.

0.8 Realization Event
A Realization Event means a sale, exchange, disposition, or other transaction that constitutes a recognition event for federal income tax purposes under existing law.

0.9 Substantially Similar Economic Exposure
Substantially Similar Economic Exposure means an arrangement in which a Covered Taxpayer retains the economic upside or downside of a Covered Asset while purporting to transfer legal title or alter formal ownership.

0.10 Covered Borrowing
Covered Borrowing means indebtedness that is secured by, guaranteed by, or economically dependent upon Covered Assets or Covered Unrealized Appreciation.

0.11 Economic Access
Economic Access means the ability, directly or indirectly, to obtain financial benefit from an asset or Covered Unrealized Appreciation without triggering a Realization Event.

0.12 Controlled Entity
A Controlled Entity means any entity in which a Covered Taxpayer, alone or together with related persons, possesses control, significant influence, or beneficial economic ownership as defined in Section 4.

0.13 Reasonable Expectation of Financial Benefit
A Reasonable Expectation of Financial Benefit exists where, based on facts and circumstances, a Covered Taxpayer can reasonably anticipate current or future economic benefit from an asset or arrangement.

0.14 Successor Asset
A Successor Asset means an asset that replaces or continues a prior asset through restructuring, transfer, exchange, or other transaction in which economic exposure is substantially preserved.



SECTION 0A — VALUATION RULES
The valuation of Covered Assets under this Framework shall rely, to the maximum extent practicable, on existing federal tax valuation concepts, fair-market-value standards, and established appraisal methods rather than creating wholly new valuation systems.

0A.1 General Rule
Except as otherwise provided in this Framework, the value of a Covered Asset shall be its fair market value as determined under existing federal tax principles applicable to estate, gift, charitable contribution, and related valuation contexts.

0A.2 Publicly Traded Securities
Publicly traded securities shall be valued using existing federal tax fair-market-value methods for exchange-traded property, including the use of quoted market prices on the applicable Measurement Date or other administratively accepted pricing conventions under current law.

0A.3 Exchange-Traded Funds, Mutual Funds, Treasuries, and Similar Instruments
Exchange-traded funds, mutual funds, Treasury securities, and similar widely traded financial instruments shall be valued using published market quotations, net asset value, or other objectively published pricing data applicable to the instrument on the relevant Measurement Date.

0A.4 Real Estate
Real estate shall be valued using existing federal tax valuation methods. A taxpayer may rely on: (a) a qualified independent appraisal prepared under standards generally applicable in federal tax administration; or (b) where administratively permitted, an objectively determined local assessed value or other government valuation metric as a rebuttable safe harbor if it reasonably approximates fair market value. A recent arm’s-length sale, purchase, or binding offer involving the property shall be given substantial weight.

0A.5 Privately Held Business Interests and Illiquid Assets
Privately held business interests and other illiquid assets shall be valued using existing federal tax fair-market-value principles and qualified appraisal practices. Where applicable, an independent appraisal prepared in a manner consistent with established federal tax valuation practice may create a rebuttable presumption of reasonableness for a limited period, absent a material intervening event affecting value.

0A.6 Arm’s-Length Transactions
A recent arm’s-length transaction involving the same asset, a substantially identical asset, or the relevant issuer or entity—including a sale, financing round, redemption, tender, acquisition, or comparable third-party transaction—shall be treated as strong evidence of value and may establish a rebuttable safe harbor where appropriate.

0A.7 Anti-Abuse Rule
No valuation safe harbor shall apply where a transaction, appraisal, restriction, transfer, or ownership arrangement lacks economic substance, is not arm’s-length, or is structured principally to understate value, avoid Deferral Cost Charges, or disrupt the operation of this Framework.

0A.8 Administrative Coordination
The Secretary shall, to the maximum extent practicable, administer this Framework in coordination with existing IRS valuation regulations, appraisal standards, penalty rules, and published guidance so that compliance burdens and valuation disputes are minimized.


SECTION 1 — COVERED TAXPAYER DETERMINATION

1.1 Scope and Purpose
The Framework applies to Covered Taxpayers as defined in this Section. It is intended to preserve realization-based taxation while ensuring alignment where economic access to appreciation occurs, particularly when deferral extends across long durations. Covered conduct includes extraction, deferral, and realization-equivalent access undertaken directly by individuals or indirectly through controlled entities, operating businesses, partnerships, trusts, or other arrangements. The presence of a business entity does not, by itself, alter the tax character of extraction or deferral.

Conversely, the Framework is not intended to tax operating activity, productive reinvestment, or ordinary business borrowing undertaken in the course of a trade or business. Determinations under the Framework are based on economic function, control, and use of proceeds, rather than formal entity classification alone.

Where individuals exercise control over assets held through entities and obtain personal economic access to appreciation—whether through borrowing, guarantees, pledges, or other realization-equivalent mechanisms—such access is treated consistently with direct ownership for purposes of aggregation, deferral pricing, and anti-abuse provisions.

Nothing in this Framework is intended to convert it into a general business tax regime. Its purpose is limited to preventing the use of entity structures to convert unrealized appreciation into indefinite, tax-free personal purchasing power.

1.2 Covered Taxpayer Tests
A household shall be treated as a Covered Taxpayer for a taxable year if one or more of the following tests is satisfied:

(A) Net Worth Test.—The household’s aggregated net worth exceeds $20,000,000 (as indexed under Section 11).

(B) Income Test.—The household’s adjusted gross income exceeds $5,000,000 for the taxable year, provided such income level reflects sustained or recurring income and is not attributable solely to a single, non-recurring spike.

Isolated or non-recurring income spikes, standing alone, do not permanently subject a household to Covered Taxpayer status.

All terms used in this Section have the meanings set forth in Section 0 (Definitions) and Section 4 (Aggregation and Look-Through).



SECTION 2 — DEFERRAL COST CHARGE

2.1 Imposition of Charge
Each taxable year, a Covered Taxpayer shall be subject to an annual Deferral Cost Charge (DCC) with respect to Covered Unrealized Appreciation (CUA) held during such year. The Deferral Cost Charge shall apply without requiring the sale, disposition, or other realization event. The Deferral Cost Charge is imposed on the continued deferral of recognition and the economic access to appreciation, and not on asset ownership as such.

2.2 Covered Unrealized Appreciation
For purposes of this Framework, Covered Unrealized Appreciation means, with respect to any Covered Asset, the excess of the fair market value of such asset over its adjusted basis, determined as of the close of the taxable year. Covered Unrealized Appreciation shall be determined after application of household aggregation, entity and trust look-through, and valuation standards prescribed under this Framework.

2.3 Rate of Charge (Progressive)
The annual Deferral Cost Charge is applied to Covered Unrealized Appreciation at the following rates:

(A) 2.0 percent on Covered Unrealized Appreciation not exceeding $25,000,000;
(B) 3.0 percent on Covered Unrealized Appreciation exceeding $25,000,000 but not exceeding $250,000,000;
(C) 4.0 percent on Covered Unrealized Appreciation exceeding $250,000,000.

2.3A Asset-Specific Time-Weighted Deferral Cost (TWDC)

Design intent.—Deferral remains permitted, but becomes progressively costly the longer a specific asset’s appreciation remains unrealized.

Core mechanic (asset-specific).—Each Covered Asset maintains its own Deferral Duration (“deferral clock”). This mechanism is asset-specific and not portfolio-averaged. Realizing (selling or otherwise triggering a Realization Event) affects only the specific asset realized.

Deferral duration and reset.—
(1) Deferral Duration begins in the first taxable year an asset becomes subject to the DCC.
(2) The deferral clock resets to zero upon a Realization Event for that asset (sale or exchange treated as realization under federal income tax law).
(3) Transfers or restructurings that preserve substantially similar economic exposure do not reset the deferral clock.

Time-weighted multiplier schedule.—Consecutive years of deferral (per asset):

| Consecutive years of deferral | TWDC multiplier |
|---|---|
| Years 1–5 | 1.00× |
| Years 6–10 | 1.25× |
| Years 11–15 | 1.50× |
| Years 16–20 | 2.00× |
| 21+ years | 2.50× |

Successor / continuation rule (anti-avoidance).—If an asset is replaced, restructured, or transferred in a manner that preserves substantially similar economic exposure, the deferral clock continues. This includes, without limitation:

• Like-kind exchanges or rollovers with continued exposure  
• Contributions to controlled entities (including partnerships, trusts, or holding companies)  
• Asset-for-asset swaps or recapitalizations lacking meaningful economic exit  
• Derivative overlays or hedged structures designed to defer realization while retaining upside  

The deferral clock resets only where the taxpayer meaningfully exits the economic position.

Policy effect.—The TWDC mechanism preserves voluntary realization decisions while preventing perpetual deferral without cost.

2.4 Credit Upon Realization
Upon the sale or other Realization Event of a Covered Asset:

(1) Capital gain shall be determined under otherwise applicable provisions of law.
(2) The taxpayer shall be allowed a credit against the tax attributable to such gain equal to the applicable portion of cumulative Deferral Cost Charges previously paid with respect to such asset, as determined under Section 2.4.1.

2.4.1 Credit Percentage (Framework Design)
Deferral Cost Charges are intended to function as a timing charge, not as an additional layer of tax. Accordingly, the portion of cumulative Deferral Cost Charges eligible for credit upon realization shall decline based on the number of taxable years the asset was subject to DCC, as follows:

Years Subject to DCC — Credit Percentage  
1–5: 100 percent  
6: 90 percent  
7: 80 percent  
8: 70 percent  
9: 60 percent  
10: 50 percent  
11: 40 percent  
12: 30 percent  
13: 20 percent  
14: 10 percent  
15 or more: 0 percent  

This declining credit structure reflects the principle that short-term deferral should not be penalized, while prolonged deferral should incur a permanent cost.

2.5 Limitation on Credit
The credit attributable to Deferral Cost Charges with respect to any Covered Asset shall not exceed the amount of federal income tax attributable to the realization of such asset for the taxable year, determined under otherwise applicable provisions of law.

No credit shall be allowed with respect to any Deferral Cost Charge attributable to a taxable year for which the applicable credit percentage under Section 2.4.1 is zero.

Any Deferral Cost Charge not credited pursuant to this Section shall not be refundable and shall not be carried forward or backward, except as may be expressly provided by implementing legislation.

2.6 Payment and Liquidity Provisions (Framework Design)
The Framework assumes mechanisms to allow Deferral Cost Charges to be satisfied without requiring forced liquidation of Covered Assets. Such mechanisms would include, at a minimum:

(1) Installment payment options, allowing Deferral Cost Charges to be paid over multiple taxable years;  
(2) In-kind payment options, permitting satisfaction of Deferral Cost Charges through the transfer of specified interests, where administratively feasible.

2.7 Anti-Avoidance
For purposes of this Section:

(1) Covered Unrealized Appreciation shall be determined on an aggregated basis across all assets and interests required to be aggregated under this Framework.
(2) Transfers of assets among related parties, entities, or trusts shall not reset the holding period or deferral duration schedule.
(3) Transactions lacking economic substance shall be disregarded.

2.8 Regulations
A legislative implementation would prescribe such regulations as are necessary to carry out the purposes of this Framework, including regulations relating to valuation methods, aggregation, credit tracking, reporting, and enforcement.

For avoidance of doubt, changes in ownership by reason of death, inheritance, or succession do not, by themselves, constitute Realization Events, reset Deferral Duration, or otherwise alter the application of Deferral Cost Charges, except as expressly provided under this Framework.



SECTION 3 — BORROWING NEUTRALITY

3.1 In General
For purposes of this Framework, borrowing shall not be treated as income. Borrowing is not treated as income under this Framework, and any tax imposed pursuant to this Section is imposed on appreciation deemed realized by reason of economic access, rather than on loan proceeds themselves.

3.2 Covered Borrowing
For purposes of this Section, Covered Borrowing means any indebtedness:

(1) Secured directly or indirectly by Covered Assets with Unrealized Appreciation; or  
(2) Guaranteed by, pledged against, or economically dependent upon Covered Assets with Unrealized Appreciation, regardless of whether such indebtedness is incurred by the Covered Taxpayer, a Controlled Entity, or a related trust.

3.3 Borrowing Neutrality Triggers
A Borrowing Neutrality Trigger shall occur for any taxable year in which either of the following thresholds is exceeded:

3.3.1 Absolute Borrowing Threshold  
Net new Covered Borrowing during the taxable year exceeds $1,000,000.

3.3.2 Appreciation-Based Threshold  
Net new Covered Borrowing during the taxable year exceeds 10 percent of Covered Unrealized Appreciation.

3.4 Leverage Ratio Trigger
A Leverage Ratio Trigger shall occur if, at any time during the taxable year, the aggregate outstanding Covered Borrowing secured by Covered Assets exceeds 30 percent of the fair market value of such assets.

3.5 Deemed Realization Upon Trigger
Upon the occurrence of any Borrowing Neutrality Trigger under Section 3.3 or any Leverage Ratio Trigger under Section 3.4:

(1) An amount of Covered Unrealized Appreciation equal to the excess borrowing shall be treated as realized gain for the taxable year.  
(2) Such gain shall be subject to tax under otherwise applicable capital gains provisions.  
(3) The adjusted basis of the applicable assets shall be increased by the amount of gain so treated as realized.

3.6 Allocation of Deemed Realization
Implementing regulations or administrative guidance would provide for the reasonable allocation of deemed realization among assets securing the borrowing, including ordering rules where multiple assets are pledged.

3.7 Anti-Avoidance
For purposes of this Section:

(1) Borrowing incurred through related parties, captive finance entities, or circular arrangements shall be treated as Covered Borrowing.  
(2) Refinancings, rollovers, or restructurings shall be treated as net new borrowing to the extent they increase economic access to untaxed appreciation.  
(3) Transfers of assets among related parties, entities, or trusts shall not reset borrowing thresholds or leverage calculations.  
(4) Transactions lacking economic substance shall be disregarded.

3.8 Regulations
A legislative implementation would prescribe such regulations as are necessary to carry out the purposes of this Section, including rules relating to aggregation, valuation, tracing of loan proceeds, and enforcement.



SECTION 4 — AGGREGATION, TRUST LOOK-THROUGH, AND CONTROL

4.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Section is to ensure that Covered Taxpayer status, Covered Unrealized Appreciation, and Covered Borrowing are determined based on economic reality and not avoided through fragmentation across households, entities, or trusts.

4.2 Household Aggregation
For purposes of this Framework, all assets, liabilities, income, appreciation, and borrowing shall be aggregated across the following persons and arrangements and treated as held by a single household:

(1) Spouses, whether filing jointly or separately;  
(2) Minor children; and  
(3) Any individual whose assets or income are substantially supported or controlled by the Covered Taxpayer.

4.3 Entity Look-Through and Control

4.3.1 Controlled Entities  
Any entity shall be treated as a Controlled Entity of a Covered Taxpayer if the Covered Taxpayer, directly or indirectly:

(1) Owns 50 percent or more of the equity, capital, profits, or voting interests; or  
(2) Exercises effective control, including through management authority, veto rights, contractual arrangements, or economic dependency.

Assets held by Controlled Entities shall be treated as held directly by the Covered Taxpayer for purposes of this Framework.

4.3.2 Attribution Through Chains  
Ownership and control shall be determined by attribution through chains of entities, partnerships, corporations, and similar arrangements, regardless of formal legal structure.

4.4 Trust Look-Through Rules

4.4.1 Covered Trusts  
A trust shall be treated as a Covered Trust with respect to a Covered Taxpayer if the Covered Taxpayer directly or indirectly:

(1) May receive distributions of income or principal;  
(2) May borrow from the trust or pledge trust assets;  
(3) May have personal expenses paid by the trust;  
(4) Retains any power of substitution, direction, consent, or veto;  
(5) Serves as trustee, co-trustee, trust protector, or holds equivalent authority; or  
(6) Otherwise exercises de facto control over trust assets or administration.

4.4.2 Attribution of Trust Assets  
Assets held by a Covered Trust shall be treated as held directly by the Covered Taxpayer for purposes of net worth determination, Covered Unrealized Appreciation, Deferral Cost Charge calculations, and Borrowing Neutrality Triggers.

4.4.3 Irrevocability Not Controlling  
The irrevocable nature of a trust shall not, by itself, prevent application of this Section if economic access or effective control exists.

4.5 Related-Party Debt and Circular Arrangements

4.5.1 Disallowed Liabilities  
Related-party debt shall be disregarded unless the Covered Taxpayer demonstrates that such debt:

(1) Is owed to an unrelated third party;  
(2) Bears commercially reasonable terms;  
(3) Is supported by actual cash transfer; and  
(4) Is serviced with regular interest payments.

4.5.2 Circular and Captive Financing  
Indebtedness involving circular cash flows, captive finance entities, back-to-back loans, or economically offsetting arrangements shall be treated as non-debt for purposes of this Framework.

4.6 Anti-Fragmentation Rule
No transaction, restructuring, division, or transfer shall be given effect if a principal purpose is to avoid application of this Framework through asset splitting among family members, use of multiple trusts, migration of assets among related arrangements, or temporary transfers lacking substantive change in control or benefit. Such transactions shall be collapsed and treated as a single arrangement.

4.7 Timing and Continuity
For purposes of Sections 2 and 3:

(1) Transfers among aggregated persons or arrangements shall not reset holding periods, credit schedules, deferral duration, or borrowing thresholds.  
(2) Covered status shall persist for not less than three taxable years after the taxpayer ceases to meet Covered Taxpayer thresholds, unless otherwise provided by regulation.

4.8 Regulatory Authority
A legislative implementation would prescribe such regulations as are necessary to carry out this Section, including attribution rules, control determinations, safe harbors for bona fide third-party arrangements, and enforcement and reporting requirements.

Substance-Based Access.—For purposes of this Framework, a trust or entity shall be treated as providing economic access where, based on facts and circumstances, the Covered Taxpayer retains meaningful ability to benefit from, direct, or influence the economic use of the assets.



SECTION 5 — VALUATION STANDARDS AND SAFE HARBORS

5.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Section is to establish clear, standardized, and administrable valuation rules for determining Covered Unrealized Appreciation, net worth, and borrowing thresholds, while providing narrowly tailored safe harbors to address liquidity constraints and reduce valuation disputes.

Anti-Artificial Basis Rule.—For purposes of determining Covered Unrealized Appreciation, basis shall reflect only amounts recognized for federal income tax purposes or otherwise explicitly recognized under this Framework. No increase in basis arising from related-party transactions, internal restructurings, recapitalizations, nonrecognition exchanges, or other arrangements that do not give rise to a taxable realization event shall be recognized for Deferral Cost Charge purposes.

Anti-Leverage Netting Rule.—Covered Unrealized Appreciation shall be determined without reduction for indebtedness incurred by the Covered Taxpayer or related parties, except to the extent such indebtedness reflects bona fide third-party acquisition cost included in basis. Indebtedness used to extract liquidity, refinance appreciated assets, fund personal consumption, or facilitate realization-equivalent access shall not reduce Covered Unrealized Appreciation for Deferral Cost Charge purposes.

Control-Overrides-Discount Rule.—Valuation discounts based on lack of marketability, minority interest, or similar factors shall not apply where the Covered Taxpayer, alone or in combination with related parties, retains control or effective control over the asset or entity, including through voting power, contractual rights, management authority, or other arrangements conferring economic control or access.

5.2 General Valuation Standard
Except as otherwise provided in this Section, assets shall be valued at fair market value, defined as the price at which the asset would change hands between a willing buyer and a willing seller, neither under compulsion and both having reasonable knowledge of relevant facts. Valuation shall be determined annually as of the close of the taxable year.

5.3 Publicly Traded Assets

5.3.1 Covered Securities  
Publicly traded securities, including stocks, bonds, exchange-traded funds, and similar instruments, shall be valued at the closing market price on the last trading day of the taxable year.

5.3.2 Derivatives  
Derivatives and similar financial instruments shall be valued based on their net economic exposure, determined under regulations set forth in implementing legislation or administrative guidance.

5.4 Real Property

5.4.1 Valuation Method  
Real property shall be valued using the greater of:  
(1) The most recent assessed value for property tax purposes; or  
(2) An automated valuation model (AVM) or comparable valuation method recognized under applicable regulations.

5.4.2 Appeals and Safe Harbor  
A taxpayer may challenge a valuation by submitting a qualified independent appraisal. No penalty shall apply if the reported value is within 15 percent of the value ultimately determined.

5.5 Closely Held Businesses and Illiquid Assets

5.5.1 Safe-Harbor Valuation Bands  
For interests in closely held businesses, private equity, venture capital, partnerships, or similar assets, valuation may be determined using safe-harbor valuation bands specified under implementing regulations, including revenue-based multiples, EBITDA-based multiples, recent arm’s-length transaction prices, or other standardized methods appropriate to the asset class.

5.5.2 Election of Appraisal  
A taxpayer may elect to use a qualified independent appraisal not more frequently than once every three taxable years, unless a material event occurs.

5.6 Primary Residence
For purposes of determining Covered Taxpayer status under Section 1.2, the first $5,000,000 of value attributable to a primary residence shall be excluded from net worth determination. Any value in excess of such amount shall be included and determined in accordance with Section 5.4.

This exclusion applies solely to the Covered Taxpayer determination and does not exclude any portion of residential property from Covered Unrealized Appreciation, the Deferral Cost Charge, or any other provision of this Framework once a household is treated as a Covered Taxpayer.

5.7 Valuation Averaging
At the election of the taxpayer, Covered Unrealized Appreciation and net worth may be determined using a three-taxable-year rolling average, provided such election applies consistently across all Covered Assets and is maintained for a minimum period prescribed by regulation.

5.8 Liquidity Safe Harbor

5.8.1 Installment Relief  
If a Covered Taxpayer demonstrates insufficient liquid assets to satisfy obligations arising under Section 2 or Section 3, payment may be made under the installment provisions set forth in Section 2.6.

5.8.2 Illiquidity Determination  
Illiquidity shall be determined based on cash and cash equivalents, readily marketable securities, and reasonably available credit from unrelated third parties.

5.9 Penalties for Systematic Undervaluation
If a taxpayer engages in repeated or systematic undervaluation outside applicable safe harbors, implementing legislation may impose appropriate penalties, adjustments, or enhanced reporting requirements.

5.10 Regulations
A legislative implementation would prescribe such regulations as are necessary to carry out this Section, including asset-class-specific valuation tables, safe-harbor thresholds and bands, appraisal standards, and reporting and documentation requirements.

Pre-Liquidity Businesses.—In the case of privately held businesses that have not undergone a public offering or other liquidity event, valuation methods shall reflect reasonable market-based estimates and shall not treat purely speculative appreciation as realized economic gain absent credible market indicators.



SECTION 6 — REPORTING, COMPLIANCE, AND ADMINISTRATION

6.1 In General
The Framework contemplates reporting, compliance, and administrative mechanisms sufficient to support accurate valuation, aggregation, and enforcement of Deferral Cost Charges and related provisions.

Reporting and compliance mechanisms are intended to scale with economic access, asset complexity, and duration of deferral, rather than imposing uniform burdens regardless of risk or scale.

Nothing in this Section is intended to require perfect valuation or eliminate reasonable estimation; rather, the objective is consistency, continuity, and verifiability over time.

6.2 Reporting by Covered Taxpayers
Covered Taxpayers shall report, on an annual basis, information sufficient to determine:

(1) Aggregated asset values and adjusted bases;  
(2) Covered Unrealized Appreciation;  
(3) Deferral Cost Charges assessed and credits accrued;  
(4) Covered Borrowing and leverage metrics; and  
(5) Changes in ownership, entity structure, or trust arrangements relevant to aggregation, look-through, or successor asset determination.

Such reporting shall reflect economic substance rather than formal legal structure.

6.3 Information Reporting by Third Parties
A legislative implementation of this Framework may include information reporting requirements applicable to financial institutions, brokers, custodians, lenders, and other intermediaries with knowledge of asset values, collateralization, borrowing, or ownership interests relevant to this Framework.

Such reporting is intended to supplement taxpayer self-reporting, improve accuracy, and reduce reliance on individualized audit determinations, consistent with existing third-party reporting regimes under federal tax law.

6.4 Recordkeeping and Documentation
Covered Taxpayers shall maintain contemporaneous documentation sufficient to substantiate valuations, borrowing arrangements, guarantees, pledges, and ownership or control relationships relevant to Covered Unrealized Appreciation and Covered Borrowing.

Implementation should permit standardized and automated recordkeeping methodologies, where commercially reasonable, to reduce compliance burden and facilitate continuity across taxable years, restructurings, and successor asset determinations.

6.5 Safe Harbors and Simplified Reporting
The Framework contemplates safe harbors and simplified reporting regimes intended to reduce compliance burden for Covered Taxpayers with relatively limited asset counts or lower aggregate Covered Unrealized Appreciation.

Any such safe harbors or simplified methods must prevent avoidance through fragmentation, restructuring, or artificial division of ownership and shall operate consistently with aggregation and look-through principles of this Framework.

6.6 Coordination With Existing Reporting Regimes
Reporting under this Framework is intended to coordinate with existing tax reporting and information regimes, including capital gains reporting, basis reporting, information returns, and foreign asset disclosures, to the extent practicable.

Nothing in this Framework is intended to duplicate reporting where substantially similar information is already provided under existing law.

6.7 Enforcement Principles
Enforcement mechanisms under a legislative implementation of this Framework should prioritize consistency, proportionality, and risk-based review.

Compliance efforts should focus on material misreporting, sustained avoidance, or misuse of deferral or borrowing mechanisms, rather than immaterial valuation differences or good-faith estimation errors.

Penalties should be proportionate to the magnitude, duration, and intent of noncompliance, consistent with the principles underlying this Framework.



SECTION 7 — COORDINATION WITH EXISTING TAX LAW

7.1 In General
The Framework is intended to operate in coordination with existing federal tax provisions, including capital gains taxation, estate and gift tax rules, trust taxation regimes, and related reporting requirements. Nothing in this Framework is intended to displace otherwise applicable tax rules except to the extent expressly provided herein.

7.2 Coordination With Capital Gains Taxation
The Framework is designed to complement, not replace, existing capital gains taxation. Amounts treated as realized under Borrowing Neutrality principles shall be taxed under otherwise applicable capital gains provisions. Deferral Cost Charges paid with respect to a Covered Asset shall be credited in accordance with Section 2.4 and Section 2.4.1, subject to applicable limitations.

Nothing in this Framework alters statutory capital gains rates, holding period rules, or character determinations, except as specifically provided.

7.3 Coordination With Estate and Gift Tax
The Framework assumes coordination with estate and gift tax provisions to prevent the elimination of Covered Unrealized Appreciation solely through transfer, inheritance, or step-up in basis at death.

Changes in ownership by reason of death, inheritance, or gift shall not, by themselves, reset Deferral Duration or eliminate accrued Deferral Cost Charges, except as expressly provided by implementing legislation.

7.4 Coordination With Trust Taxation
The Framework is intended to coordinate with existing trust taxation regimes, including grantor and non-grantor trust rules. Aggregation and look-through principles under Section 4 shall apply in determining Covered Taxpayer status, Covered Unrealized Appreciation, and Borrowing Neutrality implications involving trusts.

Nothing in this Framework is intended to alter the income taxation of trusts except to the extent necessary to prevent avoidance of Deferral Cost Charges or Borrowing Neutrality provisions through trust structures.

7.5 Avoidance of Double Taxation
The Framework is intended to prevent double taxation through consistent basis adjustments, credit mechanisms, and coordination rules.

Amounts treated as realized under Section 3 (Borrowing Neutrality) shall increase adjusted basis in the applicable assets to prevent subsequent duplication of gain recognition. Credits under Section 2.4 shall operate solely as timing adjustments and shall not create refundable or duplicative tax benefits.



SECTION 8 — TRANSITION, PHASE-IN, AND GRANDFATHERING

8.1 In General
The Framework contemplates transition rules and phase-in provisions intended to allow affected taxpayers adequate time to adjust structures, reporting systems, and liquidity planning. Transition provisions are intended to balance fairness, administrability, and avoidance prevention.

8.2 Initial Applicability
The Framework assumes that Deferral Cost Charges, Borrowing Neutrality principles, and aggregation rules would apply beginning in the first taxable year following enactment, subject to phase-in provisions described below.

8.3 Phase-In of Deferral Cost Charge
The Framework contemplates a gradual phase-in of Deferral Cost Charge rates during the initial years of implementation to reduce disruption and allow compliance systems to mature. Implementing legislation may provide reduced DCC rates for the first one to three taxable years following enactment, with full rates applying thereafter.

8.4 Treatment of Pre-Existing Assets
The Framework assumes transitional rules addressing assets held prior to implementation. Such rules may include:

(1) Establishing initial Deferral Duration for pre-existing assets beginning on the effective date;  
(2) Permitting basis verification procedures for legacy assets; and  
(3) Providing limited administrative safe harbors to address historical record limitations.

Nothing in this Section is intended to eliminate Covered Unrealized Appreciation existing as of the effective date, but rather to establish a clear starting point for Deferral Duration calculations.

8.5 Borrowing and Leverage Transition
The Framework contemplates transitional relief for borrowing arrangements existing prior to implementation. Implementing legislation may provide:

(1) A grace period during which pre-existing borrowing is not treated as triggering Deemed Realization;  
(2) Gradual application of Borrowing Neutrality thresholds; and  
(3) Refinancing safe harbors, provided such refinancing does not materially increase economic access to untaxed appreciation.

8.6 Anti-Abuse During Transition
The Framework assumes anti-abuse provisions to prevent acceleration, restructuring, artificial transfers, or borrowing designed to avoid application prior to or during the transition period.

Transactions undertaken with a principal purpose of avoiding transition rules may be recharacterized consistent with the substance-over-form principles underlying this Framework.



SECTION 9 — ANTI-AVOIDANCE AND ECONOMIC SUBSTANCE

9.1 In General
The Framework is intended to be applied consistent with principles of economic substance, substance-over-form, and existing judicial anti-abuse doctrines. Formal compliance with structural rules shall not control where economic reality demonstrates extraction, control, or indefinite deferral inconsistent with the purposes of this Framework.

9.2 Disregard of Artificial Transactions
Transactions, arrangements, or series of steps that lack economic substance, are undertaken primarily for tax avoidance, or artificially manipulate valuation, aggregation, borrowing thresholds, or Deferral Duration shall be disregarded or recharacterized in accordance with their economic substance.

9.3 Step Transaction Doctrine
Related transactions undertaken pursuant to a plan, understanding, or series of steps shall be treated as a single transaction where necessary to reflect economic reality and prevent avoidance of Deferral Cost Charges or Borrowing Neutrality provisions.

9.4 Circular and Offset Arrangements
Circular, reciprocal, offsetting, or self-canceling arrangements that obscure economic ownership, shift appreciation without meaningful change in control, or manufacture artificial basis or leverage shall be collapsed and treated according to their underlying economic effect.

9.5 Use of Intermediaries
The use of intermediaries, conduits, or nominal parties shall not prevent application of aggregation, look-through, or Borrowing Neutrality principles where the Covered Taxpayer retains economic access, control, or effective ownership.

9.6 Coordination With Existing Anti-Abuse Doctrines
The Framework is intended to operate consistently with existing judicial and statutory anti-abuse doctrines, including economic substance rules, sham transaction doctrine, and related-party attribution principles. Nothing in this Section is intended to limit broader anti-abuse authority under existing law.

9.7 Borrowing Character Test and Misclassification Penalties
Borrowing secured by appreciated assets is not inherently abusive and remains neutral where used to support the productive operation of the collateral. Borrowing becomes realization-relevant only to the extent that proceeds are used to extract economic value unrelated to the collateral’s operating activity.

Where borrowing functions as a substitute for realization, accessed appreciation shall be treated under applicable Deferral Cost Charge or Deemed Realization provisions to prevent indefinite deferral from functioning as a permanent substitute for taxation.

Borrowing shall be presumed extractive where proceeds fund:

(1) Personal consumption;  
(2) Acquisition of unrelated assets or entities;  
(3) Control transactions;  
(4) Portfolio or lifestyle leverage; or  
(5) Non-amortizing or indefinitely rolled borrowing arrangements primarily designed to sustain liquidity without realization.

Misclassification of extractive borrowing as operating borrowing shall result in retroactive recharacterization to the original borrowing date, application of enhanced Deferral Cost Charges where appropriate, penalty surcharges sufficient to eliminate any economic benefit from misclassification, and potential limitation or temporary suspension of eligibility for safe harbor treatment.

Penalties under this subsection are intended to deter deliberate misclassification and advisory-driven gaming, while preserving legitimate operating borrowing used in productive business activity.



SECTION 10 — EFFECTIVE DATE, REVIEW, AND EVOLUTION

10.1 Effective Date
The Framework contemplates an effective date sufficient to allow for orderly implementation, system preparation, regulatory guidance, and taxpayer compliance planning. Implementing legislation may provide delayed effectiveness or staged applicability as necessary to ensure administrability.

10.2 Periodic Review
The Framework assumes periodic review of thresholds, rates, valuation standards, credit schedules, and compliance mechanisms to ensure continued alignment with economic conditions and policy objectives.

Such review should occur at regular intervals and may include adjustments to ensure proportionality, neutrality, and effectiveness in preventing indefinite deferral without unintended distortion.

10.3 Data-Driven Evaluation
A legislative implementation of this Framework would include mechanisms for collecting and analyzing data relevant to Deferral Duration patterns, Borrowing Neutrality triggers, compliance outcomes, and revenue effects.

Periodic public reporting may be used to assess whether the Framework continues to achieve its structural objectives without excessive administrative burden or market disruption.

10.4 Amendments and Refinement
The Framework is intended to serve as a living design, capable of refinement and adjustment over time based on empirical evidence, administrative experience, and economic evolution. Nothing in this Section limits future legislative modification consistent with the principles underlying this Framework.


SECTION 11 — INFLATION ADJUSTMENT

11.1 In General
Any dollar amount specified in this Framework shall be adjusted annually for inflation.

11.2 Method of Adjustment
Adjustments shall be made by multiplying the applicable dollar amount by the ratio of the Consumer Price Index (CPI-U) for the preceding calendar year to the CPI-U for the base year specified in implementing legislation.

11.3 Rounding
Any amount so adjusted shall be rounded to the nearest $100,000, or such other rounding increment as may be specified by implementing legislation.
