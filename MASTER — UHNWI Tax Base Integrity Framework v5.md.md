SECTION 1 — COVERED TAXPAYER DETERMINATION

1.1 The Framework applies to the
preservation of the tax base wherever economic access to appreciation occurs. Covered conduct includes extraction, deferral, and realization-equivalent access undertaken directly by individuals or indirectly through controlled entities, operating businesses, partnerships, trusts, or other arrangements. The presence of a business entity does not, by itself, alter the tax character of extraction or deferral. Conversely, the Framework is not intended to tax operating activity, productive reinvestment, or ordinary business borrowing undertaken in the course of a trade or business. Determinations under the Framework are based on economic function, control, and use of proceeds, rather than formal entity classification alone. Where individuals exercise control over assets held through entities and obtain personal economic access to appreciation--whether through borrowing, guarantees, pledges, or other realization-equivalent mechanisms--such access is treated consistently with direct ownership for purposes of aggregation, deferral pricing, and anti-abuse provisions. Nothing in this Framework is intended to convert it into a general business tax regime. Its purpose is limited to preventing the use of entity structures to convert unrealized appreciation into indefinite, tax-free personal purchasing power.

1.2 A household shall be treated as a Covered Taxpayer for a taxable year if one or more of the following tests is satisfied:
(A) Net Worth Test.—The household’s aggregated net worth exceeds $20,000,000 (as indexed). (B) Income Test.—The household’s adjusted gross income exceeds $5,000,000 for the taxable year, provided such income level reflects sustained or recurring income and is not attributable solely to a single, non-recurring spike. Isolated or non-recurring income spikes, standing alone, do not permanently subject a household to Covered Taxpayer status. All terms used in this Section have the meanings set forth in Section 0 (Definitions) and Section 4 (Aggregation and Look-Through).

SECTION 3 — BORROWING NEUTRALITY
3.1 In General
For purposes of this Framework, borrowing shall not be treated as income. Borrowing is not treated as income under this Framework, and any tax imposed pursuant to this sect imposed on appreciation deemed realized by reason of economic access, rather than on loan proceeds.

3.2 Covered Borrowing
For purposes of this section, Covered Borrowing means any indebtedness: (1) Secured directly or indirectly by covered appreciated assets, or (2) Guaranteed by, pledged against, or economically dependent upon covered appreciated assets, regardless of whether such indebtedness is incurred by the Covered Taxpayer, a controlled entity, related trust.
3.3 Borrowing Neutrality Triggers
A Borrowing Neutrality Trigger shall occur for any taxable year in which either of the following t is exceeded: 3.3.1 Absolute Borrowing Threshold Net new Covered Borrowing during the taxable year exceeds $1,000,000. 3.3.2 Appreciation-Based Threshold Net new Covered Borrowing during the taxable year exceeds 10 percent of Covered Unrealized Appreciation.
3.4 Leverage Ratio Trigger
A Leverage Ratio Trigger shall occur if, at any time during the taxable year, the aggregate outsta Covered Borrowing secured by covered appreciated assets exceeds 30 percent of the fair market valu of such assets.
3.5 Deemed Realization Upon Trigger
Upon the occurrence of any Borrowing Neutrality Trigger under Section 3.3 or any Leverage Ratio Tr under Section 3.4: (1) An amount of Covered Unrealized Appreciation equal to the excess borrowing shall be treated as realized gain for the taxable year. (2) Such gain shall be subject to tax under otherwise applicable capital gains provisions. (3) The adjusted basis of the applicable assets shall be increased by the amount of gain so treate realized.

3.7 Anti-Avoidance

(2) Refinancings, rollovers, or restructurings shall be treated as net new borrowing to the extent increase economic access to untaxed appreciation. SECTION 4 — AGGREGATION, TRUST LOOK-THROUGH, AND CONTROL
4.1 Purpose
The purpose of this section is to ensure that Covered Taxpayer status, Covered Unrealized Apprecia and Covered Borrowing are determined based on economic reality, and not avoided through fragmentation across households, entities, or trusts.
4.2 Household Aggregation
For purposes of this Framework, all assets, liabilities, income, appreciation, and borrowing shall across the following persons and arrangements and treated as held by a single household: (1) Spouses, whether filing jointly or separately; (2) Minor children; and (3) Any individual whose assets or income are substantially supported or controlled by the Covered Taxpayer.
4.3 Entity Look-Through and Control
4.3.1 Controlled Entities Any entity shall be treated as a controlled entity of a Covered Taxpayer if the Covered Taxpayer,

or indirectly: (1) Owns 50 percent or more of the equity, capital, profits, or voting interests; or (2) Exercises effective control, including through management authority, veto rights, contractual arrangements, or economic dependency. Assets held by controlled entities shall be treated as held directly by the Covered Taxpayer for p this Framework. 4.3.2 Attribution Through Chains Ownership and control shall be determined by attribution through chains of entities, partnerships, corporations, and similar arrangements, regardless of formal legal structure.
4.4 Trust Look-Through Rules
4.4.1 Covered Trusts A trust shall be treated as a covered trust with respect to a Covered Taxpayer if the Covered Taxp directly or indirectly: (1) May receive distributions of income or principal; (2) May borrow from the trust or pledge trust assets; (3) May have personal expenses paid by the trust; (4) Retains any power of substitution, direction, consent, or veto; (5) Serves as trustee, co-trustee, trust protector, or holds equivalent authority; or (6) Otherwise exercises de facto control over trust assets or administration. 4.4.2 Attribution of Trust Assets Assets held by a covered trust shall be treated as held directly by the Covered Taxpayer for purpo net worth determination, Covered Unrealized Appreciation, Deferral Cost Charge, and Borrowing Neutrality Triggers. 4.4.3 Irrevocability Not Controlling The irrevocable nature of a trust shall not, by itself, prevent application of this section if eco or control exists.

4.5.1 Disallowed Liabilities Related-party debt shall be disregarded unless the Covered Taxpayer demonstrates that such debt is by an unrelated third party, bears commercially reasonable terms, is supported by actual cash tran and is serviced with regular interest payments. 4.5.2 Circular and Captive Financing Indebtedness involving circular cash flows, captive finance entities, back-to-back loans, or econo offsetting arrangements shall be treated as non-debt for purposes of this Framework.

No transaction, restructuring, division, or transfer shall be given effect if a principal purpose application of this Framework through asset splitting among family members, use of multiple trusts migration of assets among related arrangements, or temporary transfers lacking substantive change control or benefit. SECTION 5 — VALUATION STANDARDS AND SAFE HARBORS
5.1 Purpose

Anti-Artificial Basis Rule.
For purposes of determining Covered Unrealized Appreciation, basis shall reflect only amounts recognized for federal income tax purposes or otherwise explicitly recognized under this Framework. No increase in basis arising from related-party transactions, internal restructurings, recapitalizations, nonrecognition exchanges, or other arrangements that do not give rise to a taxable realization event shall be recognized for Deferral Cost Charge purposes.

Anti-Leverage Netting Rule.
Covered Unrealized Appreciation shall be determined without reduction for indebtedness incurred by the Covered Taxpayer or related parties, except to the extent such indebtedness reflects bona fide third-party acquisition cost included in basis. Indebtedness used to extract liquidity, refinance appreciated assets, fund personal consumption, or facilitate realization-equivalent access shall not reduce Covered Unrealized Appreciation for Deferral Cost Charge purposes.

Control-Overrides-Discount Rule.
Valuation discounts based on lack of marketability, minority interest, or similar factors shall not apply where the Covered Taxpayer, alone or in combination with related parties, retains control or effective control over the asset or entity, including through voting power, contractual rights, management authority, or other arrangements conferring economic control or access.

The purpose of this section is to establish clear, standardized, and administrable valuation rules

determining Covered Unrealized Appreciation, net worth, and borrowing thresholds, while providing narrowly tailored safe harbors to address liquidity constraints and reduce valuation disputes.
5.2 General Valuation Standard
Except as otherwise provided in this section, assets shall be valued at fair market value, defined price at which the asset would change hands between a willing buyer and a willing seller, neither under compulsion and both having reasonable knowledge of relevant facts. Valuation shall be determ annually as of the close of the taxable year.
